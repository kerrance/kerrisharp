Reviews

Included below are reviews and opinions on things that I have been thinking about recently.

Books

  • "Talks With a Devil" by P.D. OuspenskyTwo allegorical stories about the nature of the devil from the Slavic mythos. Devils thrive from our materialism and rationalisations.
  • "A State of Fear: How the UK government weaponised fear during the Covid-19 pandemic" by Laura DodsworthAn important document detailing the government and media's methods to exploit psychology and illicit fear in order to coerce the UK population.
  • "The Clean Coder: A Code of Conduct for Professional Programmers" by Robert C. Martin (Uncle Bob)A must-read for any software programmer. If you've ever been, or wish to be paid to write code then this book is for you.
  • "A Tale for the Time Being" by Ruth OzekiMeditations on Zen Buddhism through the lens of a tale of two souls, connected by a tragic real world event.
  • "12 Rules for Life: An Antidote to Chaos" by Jordan B PetersonInsightful meditations on modern life with classic wisdom. Jordan Peterson is the Father most of us never had.
  • "Tragedy and Hope 101: The Illusion of Justice, Freedom, and Democracy" by Joseph PlummerQuigley's Tragedy & Hope is a notoriously long and difficult book to read: Plummer does a great job of breaking it down and adding his own insight into how a cabal technocratic elites gained and maintain control.
  • "Animal Farm" by George OrwellOrwell's allegory for the Russian Revolution won't be forgotten easily. Animal Farm is short, yet it contains many similar themes and concepts to Orwell's other well-known dystopia novel. Animal Farm is a fantastic way to teach the brutal concepts of socialism in action to children or young adults.
  • "The Life-Changing Magic of Tidying" by Marie KondōTruly joyful to read. Maybe not for everyone, but Kondo presents a tried and true method for cutting down your possessions and living in a cleaner environment. I've not read a book before which makes me feel so clean and my mind so tidy, every time I flick through it. Even touching this book for me "sparks joy", I still dip back into this every now and then.

Music

  • "Escapades" by Gaspard AugeExpanding on Justice's more recent prog-rock output, "Escapades" collates the Italian horror soundtrack sound with epic video game adventure vibes.
  • "Miss Anthropocene" by GrimesThematically mixed concept album. There's some brilliant industrial and electro songs here, but the concept falls flat by the end.
  • "ネオ東京" by マクロスMACROSS 82-99Summer-time jams from both the past and the future. This quintessential 'future funk' album won't fail to put a smile on your face.

Movies

  • "Evangelion: 3.0+1.0 Thrice Upon a Time" on Amazon PrimeA true end for Evangelion, showing growth in Anno's younger otaku world-view. Humanity isn't a hedgehog's dilemma when given hope.

Exercise

  • Beginners Brazilian Jiu-jitsuRoll around and try to grapple your opponent until submission. I have no idea what I'm doing, but it's fun, physically demanding exercise.
  • Hannah Frankson's Peloton ClassesGo hard, ride fast and sweat buckets! Hannah's classes are wall-to-wall, bass-heavy British club bangers.

Games

  • "Death Stranding" on PCJeffrey Bezos and Daryl from The Walking Dead attempt to make American great again with Fedex, unborn babies and wireless internet.
  • "Hynospace Outlaw" on PCA weird and wonderful simulation of the wild-west, late 90s internet 1.0.
  • "Paratopic" on PCA lo-fi indie horror game with an eerie PS1-era aesthetic. If Silent Hill was directed by David Lynch.
  • "Mario Kart 8 Deluxe" on Nintendo SwitchSilky smooth, saccharine multiplayer fun in HD. Nintendo actually made the confectionery kart game from "Wreck It Ralph" a reality.
